The ELK Stack: Centralized Logging

The ELK Stack is a powerful combination of three open-source tools for centralized logging, log analysis, and visualization.

  • Elasticsearch: A distributed search and analytics engine. It stores all the logs.
  • Logstash: A server-side data processing pipeline that ingests data from multiple sources, transforms it, and sends it to a "stash" like Elasticsearch.
  • Kibana: A data visualization dashboard that works on top of Elasticsearch, allowing you to create charts, graphs, and dashboards to view your log data.

Note on Log Shippers: In many of my projects, I use Filebeat instead of the full Logstash agent on the client-side. Filebeat is a lightweight, resource-friendly log shipper ("Beat") designed to send logs to either Logstash for further processing or directly to Elasticsearch. This is often more efficient for simple log forwarding from many servers.
